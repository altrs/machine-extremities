MACHINE EXTREMITIES

/

1

Our desire for innovation and continuous technological advancement stem from a desire for growth. Likewise, growth is often spurred because we feel some type of inadequacy.

2. Therefore, in our endeavours of growth and advancement, our technologies often encapsulate and expose those very inadequacies we are trying to leave behind.

3

These inadequacies often suggest a point of shame that is driving the technology/innovation.

4

While shame is not necessarily negative, it is a reactionary emotion that, if left unchecked, can become dangerous, as inadequacy highly-steeped in shame creates untethered dissatisfaction.

5

Untethered dissatisfaction can lead to technologies that can place ourselves in a never-ending and unrealistic cycle of escaping inadequacy, often resulting in technology that is hyperfocused on a small set of desires often ignoring all else.

6

In turn, the trajectories of our technology become increasingly controlling, exploitative, neurotic and insidious as both creator and users cope with never-ending cycle shame.

7

By acknowledging the presence of shame within our technologies, we can start to better understand why technologies that set out with good intentions, may turn sour, driving us further and further away from ourselves and initial goals.

8

This web publication suggests a humility-based design innovation framework to compliment and guide our feelings of shame, subsequently guiding our technologies and fostering well-rounded innovation.
